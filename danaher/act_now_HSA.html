Have a Health Savings Account (HSA) now? Know what you need to do to easily move your HSA balance, if you have one

Danaher’s contribution

  • How Danaher contributes to the cost of your medical coverage will work differently in 2019.
  • You’ll receive the same “subsidy” amount from the company, but how it’s applied will change. It will go toward reducing the amount you pay per paycheck for your medical coverage.
  • With this change, Danaher will not contribute to your HSA in 2019.
  • If you enroll in a low-cost Silver plan, you could end up paying less out of your paycheck than you do today.

Take the opportunity to “get ahead” on expenses

  • Since you’re already used to a certain amount of money coming out of your paycheck, consider “reinvesting” your money into your HSA. You’ll see minimal (if any) impact to your paycheck if you contribute:
    • $500 for associate only coverage
    • $1,000 for associate + family* coverage (*2 or more people)
  • But don’t stop there! Take it to the next level by contributing enough to cover your medical plan deductible. Or anywhere in between.
  • If you choose, you can contribute all the way up to the IRS maximum (if you’re under age 55—$3,500 for associate only coverage/$7,000 for associate + family coverage, or if over 55, $4,500 and $8,000).
  • Use the online tools when you enroll to decide the right amount to contribute.

New HSA bank

  • The bank that manages our HSA is changing from Optum to Fidelity.
  • If you currently have a balance in your HSA with Optum, check the box to accept the terms and easily move your balance to Fidelity when you enroll. That way your HSA money is all in one place.
  • It’s your choice to move the money or not—just know that it’s more complicated after November 20 and you’ll have to pay a $20 transfer fee if you decide to move it later
