Top 3 Things to Know

This year is different. Three things you need to know for enrollment November 1 – 20

New medical plan choicess

Good news—several medical insurance carriers will be available for you to choose from. More choice means you have the power to find a plan that’s truly best for you.

Find out why having more medical insurance carriers is a good thing.[Link to Choose Your Medical Insurance Carrier]

Overwhelmed by options? Don’t worry! Use the Help Me Choose tool when you enroll to make it easier to see the best fit for you.

FYI, most people will continue to have two medical plan options—with new names:

  • The Health Plus Plan in 2018 is now closest to the Silver Plan in 2019.
  • The Basic Plan in 2018 is now closest to the Gold Plan in 2019.


If you want more detail, you can see a side-by-side comparison.

Note: There are differences if you live in California, Hawaii, and for U.S. Expatriates.

Updates to the Health Savings Account (HSA)

The HSA is a great way to save money on health care expenses! And now, if you enroll in the lowest-cost Silver plan, you’ll be “ahead” on your health care spend, which gives you a nice starting point for contributing to an HSA.

Note that one reason your medical plan may cost less in 2019 is because Danaher is contributing money (the company subsidy) toward your medical plan rather than your HSA.

Also, this is important: If you have an existing HSA with Optum, the bank is changing to Fidelity. You need to take action to move your account during Annual Enrollment if you want to avoid hassle and fees later.

Learn more about the HSA changes, or get a refresher on how the HSA works.

New way to enroll

MyDanaherBenefits.comis replacing the MyBenefits.MyDanaher.com site. The names are similar, so make note (and bookmark) the new site to make sure you’re in the right place.
