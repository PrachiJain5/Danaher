Choose your medical insurance carrier

Watch this for tips on what to consider when choosing a medical carrier.

This year, you have the power to choose the medical carrier you want to support you.

You’ll be able to see the carriers available to you when you enroll. Depending on where you live, your options may include:

  • Aetna
  • CareFirst (BlueCross BlueShield)
  • Cigna
  • UnitedHealthcare
  • Dean / Prevea 360 (generally available in WI)
  • Geisinger (generally available in PA)
  • Health Net (generally available in CA and OR)
  • Kaiser Permanente (generally available in CA, CO, DC, GA, MD, VA, OR, WA)
  • Medical Mutual (generally available in OH)
  • UPMC Health Plan (generally available in PA)

U.S. Expatriates

If you are on assignment outside the U.S. and are paid by the OpCo’s U.S. Payroll, Aetna International will be your medical insurance carrier. Learn more

If you live in California

You’ll be able to choose from Aetna, CareFirst, Cigna, Health Net, Kaiser Permanente, and UnitedHealthcare. Your plans will also be slightly different, depending on the carrier you choose. Learn more

If you live in Hawaii

New: Kaiser Permanente and HMSA will be available to you in 2019. Your current medical insurance carrier—UnitedHealthcare—will no longer be an option for you.
Your plans will also be different. Learn more

Prescription drugs

Your prescription drug coverage in 2019 depends on the medical plan you choose and your medical insurance carrier:

  • CVS Caremark will continue to manage your prescription drug benefits if you enroll in a medical plan through Aetna, CareFirst, Cigna, or UnitedHealthcare
  • A different company will manage your prescription drug benefits if you enroll in a medical plan through Kaiser Permanente, Health Net, Dean / Prevea360, Geisinger, Medical Mutual or UPMC.
  • se the prescription drug search tool when you enroll to see how your medication is covered under each plan.

Quick tips for choosing

Here are a few smart questions to consider when comparing the medical insurance carriers:

  1. Who’s in network? Which carriers include my doctors?


    2. Note they all have quality doctors, hospitals, urgent care clinics, and more that offer you discounts. When you enroll, use the tools to see which carriers include your doctors and the hospitals or other facilities you like to go to.

  2. What does my prescription drug coverage look like? How much will my medications cost? Is my local pharmacy in the network?


    If you or a covered family member regularly takes medication, find out what to pay attention to. When you enroll, you’ll be able to see how your specific medications will be covered.

  3. What other services does the carrier offer? What would help me get healthy and/or stay healthy?


    4. Compare the “extras” a carrier might offer, including:

    • Telemedicine
    • Health experts
    • Discount programs
    • Extended customer service hours
    • Mobile apps and tools
    • Mental health resources (in addition to services available through Danaher’s Employee Assistance Program)

  4. How much will it cost me?

    Each carrier sets its own price per paycheck for the plan you select. The Pricing Modeler [open tool] shows who’s offering you the best deal, and how much it will cost. You’ll need an access code to use it—find the code that was sent to you via email, or in the printed guide mailed home.*

    *Note the Pricing Modeler is not available to associates in Hawaii or U.S. Expatriates.

Is your address up to date? Your specific options are based on where you live—so it’s important to make sure your address in Workday is correct before you enroll.
